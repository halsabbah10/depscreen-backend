# CANMAT 2016 Pharmacotherapy Recommendations for MDD

## First-Line Agents (Level 1-2 Evidence)

All first-line agents have demonstrated superior efficacy to placebo with acceptable tolerability in multiple RCTs.

### SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors)
- **Citalopram** (20-40 mg/day): Well-tolerated; QTc prolongation at higher doses limits max to 40 mg (20 mg in elderly)
- **Escitalopram** (10-20 mg/day): S-enantiomer of citalopram; slightly better efficacy and tolerability in network meta-analyses; CANMAT and Lancet meta-analysis top-ranked
- **Fluoxetine** (20-60 mg/day): Long half-life (advantage for missed doses, disadvantage for drug interactions); only SSRI FDA-approved for adolescent depression
- **Fluvoxamine** (100-300 mg/day): More CYP interactions; less commonly used for MDD (more for OCD); included in CANMAT first-line
- **Paroxetine** (20-50 mg/day): Potent; higher discontinuation syndrome risk; some anticholinergic effects; weight gain more common
- **Sertraline** (50-200 mg/day): Strong safety data in cardiac patients and perinatal use; well-tolerated; first-line choice across multiple guidelines

### SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors)
- **Desvenlafaxine** (50-100 mg/day): Active metabolite of venlafaxine; fewer CYP interactions; flat dose-response above 50 mg
- **Duloxetine** (60-120 mg/day): Dual indication for depression and neuropathic pain/fibromyalgia; useful when pain is comorbid
- **Venlafaxine XR** (75-225 mg/day): Dose-dependent norepinephrine activity; monitor blood pressure at higher doses; evidence for treatment-resistant depression

### Other First-Line Agents
- **Agomelatine** (25-50 mg/day): Melatonergic (MT1/MT2) agonist + 5-HT2C antagonist; favorable sexual side-effect profile; requires liver function monitoring (ALT/AST at baseline, 3, 6, 12, 24 weeks)
- **Bupropion XL** (150-300 mg/day): Norepinephrine-dopamine reuptake inhibitor; no sexual dysfunction or weight gain; contraindicated in seizure disorders and eating disorders; activating profile useful for fatigue/hypersomnia
- **Mirtazapine** (15-45 mg/day): NaSSA; sedating at lower doses (useful for insomnia); appetite stimulation / weight gain; fewer GI side effects than SSRIs; complementary mechanism for combination strategies
- **Vortioxetine** (10-20 mg/day): Multimodal serotonergic; evidence for cognitive benefit in MDD (FOCUS trial); first-line in CANMAT; may cause nausea

## Second-Line Agents (Level 2-3 Evidence)

Used when first-line agents are ineffective, not tolerated, or contraindicated.

- **TCAs** (e.g., amitriptyline, nortriptyline, imipramine): Effective but higher side-effect burden (anticholinergic, sedation, cardiac conduction, weight gain); lethal in overdose; require blood level monitoring for nortriptyline
- **Quetiapine XR** (50-300 mg/day): Atypical antipsychotic with antidepressant properties at low doses; metabolic monitoring required (weight, glucose, lipids); sedation common
- **Trazodone** (150-300 mg/day): Serotonin antagonist/reuptake inhibitor; at lower doses (25-100 mg) primarily used as sleep aid in depression; priapism risk (rare); orthostatic hypotension

## Third-Line Agents (Level 3-4 Evidence)

Reserved for treatment-resistant cases under specialist supervision.

- **MAOIs** (phenelzine 45-90 mg/day, tranylcypromine 30-60 mg/day): Potent efficacy including atypical depression; strict dietary tyramine restrictions; serious drug interactions (serotonin syndrome with SSRIs/SNRIs — mandatory 2-week washout); reserved for refractory depression
- **Reboxetine** (8-12 mg/day): Selective norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor; modest efficacy; not available in all countries; insomnia, dry mouth, urinary hesitancy common

## Prescribing Principles

1. **Start at recommended therapeutic dose** — sub-therapeutic dosing delays response
2. **Allow adequate trial duration**: 4-6 weeks at therapeutic dose before declaring non-response
3. **Titrate to maximum tolerated dose** before switching, unless side effects are intolerable
4. **Discuss realistic timelines**: Onset of benefit 2-4 weeks; full effect may take 6-8 weeks
5. **Side-effect management**: Proactive discussion improves adherence; many side effects (nausea, headache, activation) are transient (1-2 weeks)
6. **Medication selection factors**: Prior response, family member response, comorbidities, side-effect profile, drug interactions, cost, patient preference

## Network Meta-Analysis Context (Cipriani et al., Lancet 2018)

Among 21 antidepressants, escitalopram, mirtazapine, paroxetine, agomelatine, and sertraline ranked highest for combined efficacy and acceptability. This supports CANMAT first-line recommendations.
