# Measurement-Based Care for Depression Using PHQ-9

## What Is Measurement-Based Care?

Measurement-based care (MBC) uses standardized, validated tools to systematically track symptom severity and treatment response at regular intervals. It replaces subjective clinical impression ("How are you feeling?") with quantified data that drives treatment decisions.

Evidence shows MBC doubles remission rates compared to standard care (Guo et al., 2015) and was a core methodology in the STAR*D trial.

## PHQ-9 as the Primary Tracking Tool

### Severity Thresholds
| PHQ-9 Score | Severity | Clinical Interpretation |
|-------------|----------|------------------------|
| 0-4 | Minimal / None | Remission range |
| 5-9 | Mild | Monitor; consider low-intensity intervention |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Active treatment indicated |
| 15-19 | Moderately Severe | Combined treatment recommended |
| 20-27 | Severe | Intensive treatment; safety assessment |

### Key Properties
- 9 items mapping directly to DSM-5 MDD criteria
- Each item scored 0-3 (not at all → nearly every day)
- Item 9 screens for suicidal ideation (any score >0 requires clinical follow-up)
- Validated sensitivity (88%) and specificity (88%) for MDD at cutoff ≥10
- Available in 80+ languages; free to use without license

## Recommended Assessment Schedule

### Acute Phase (Weeks 0-12)
| Timepoint | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Baseline (Week 0)** | Establish PHQ-9 score before treatment initiation. Document severity category. |
| **Week 2** | Early tolerability check. PHQ-9 not expected to change much yet. Assess side effects, adherence. |
| **Week 4** | First critical decision point. Calculate percentage change from baseline. |
| **Week 8** | Second decision point. If not responding, treatment modification needed. |
| **Week 12** | End of acute phase. Determine if response/remission achieved. |

### Continuation Phase (Months 4-12)
- Monthly PHQ-9 assessments
- Watch for relapse (PHQ-9 increase of ≥5 points or return above 10)
- Maintain effective treatment for minimum 4-9 months after achieving remission

### Maintenance Phase (12+ Months)
- PHQ-9 every 2-3 months
- Continue for ≥1 year for recurrent depression (≥2 episodes)
- Continue for ≥2 years for highly recurrent depression (≥3 episodes)

## Response Categories and Clinical Actions

### Remission: PHQ-9 < 5
- **Goal achieved.** Continue current treatment through continuation phase.
- Do not stop treatment prematurely — relapse risk is highest in the first 6 months after remission.
- Transition to less frequent monitoring.

### Response: ≥50% Reduction from Baseline
- Treatment is working. Continue current approach.
- If not yet at remission, optimize (dose increase, add psychotherapy, address residual symptoms).
- Example: Baseline PHQ-9 = 18, current = 8 → 56% reduction = response. Target: <5.

### Partial Response: 25-49% Reduction from Baseline
- Some improvement, but insufficient. Action needed.
- Options: optimize dose, add augmentation, add psychotherapy, reassess comorbidities.
- Do not wait indefinitely — adjust by Week 8 at latest.

### Non-Response: <25% Reduction from Baseline
- Current treatment is not working adequately.
- Verify adherence, adequate dose, correct diagnosis.
- Consider switching medication or class, adding augmentation, or changing approach entirely.
- Refer for specialist evaluation if two adequate trials have failed.

## Tracking Individual Symptom Clusters

Beyond the total score, PHQ-9 items can reveal specific symptom patterns:

| Items | Cluster | Clinical Relevance |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Core mood (anhedonia, depressed mood) | If these improve last, consider activation-focused interventions |
| 3 | Sleep | Persistent insomnia may need targeted intervention (CBT-I, trazodone) |
| 4 | Fatigue | If residual, consider bupropion augmentation |
| 5 | Appetite | Direction matters: increase vs. decrease suggests different phenotypes |
| 6 | Guilt/worthlessness | Core cognitive symptom; CBT particularly effective |
| 7 | Concentration | If residual, assess for ADHD comorbidity; vortioxetine may help |
| 8 | Psychomotor changes | Agitation vs. retardation have different treatment implications |
| 9 | Suicidal ideation | Any score >0 requires immediate clinical assessment |

## Implementation in Practice

1. **Administer PHQ-9 before every visit** — patient completes in waiting room or via patient portal
2. **Plot scores over time** — visual trajectory is more informative than any single score
3. **Share results with the patient** — increases engagement, validates experience, supports shared decision-making
4. **Document in medical record** — creates objective treatment history for future providers
5. **Use algorithm-driven decision-making** — remove subjectivity from "is this working?" determinations

## Common Pitfalls

- **Collecting scores without acting on them**: MBC only works if scores trigger clinical decisions
- **Over-relying on a single timepoint**: Trajectory matters more than any single score
- **Ignoring Item 9**: Every non-zero score on suicidal ideation requires follow-up, even if the patient seems fine
- **Stopping measurement after remission**: Relapse monitoring requires ongoing assessment
