# NICE NG222: Depression in Adults — Overview

## Background

NICE guideline NG222 (2022) replaced the earlier CG90 and provides updated recommendations for the treatment and management of depression in adults (18+) in England and Wales. It introduced a stepped-care model that matches treatment intensity to clinical need, emphasizing less intensive interventions first and stepping up when needed.

## Stepped-Care Model Summary

The NICE stepped-care framework organizes depression treatment into escalating levels of intensity:

| Step | Focus | Setting | Target Population |
|------|-------|---------|------------------|
| 1 | Recognition, assessment, and initial management | Primary care | All presentations |
| 2 | Low-intensity psychological interventions | Primary care / IAPT | Less severe depression (new episode, mild-moderate) |
| 3 | High-intensity psychological interventions and/or medication | Primary care / specialist | Moderate-severe, or inadequate response to Step 2 |
| 4 | Specialist treatment, crisis services, inpatient care | Secondary/tertiary care | Severe, treatment-resistant, risk to life |

## Key Principles

### 1. Recognition in Primary Care
- Use validated screening tools (PHQ-2 → PHQ-9) for case identification
- Be alert to depression in patients with chronic physical health conditions (diabetes, cardiovascular disease, chronic pain) where prevalence is 2-3x higher
- Consider depression when patients present with unexplained somatic complaints, fatigue, or functional decline
- Ask about psychosocial stressors, substance use, and prior mental health history

### 2. Person-Centered Care
- Discuss all available treatment options with the patient
- Provide information in an accessible format (language, literacy level, cultural context)
- Respect patient preference when choosing between equally effective treatments
- Involve family/carers when appropriate and with the patient's consent
- Address barriers to engagement (childcare, transport, work schedules, digital access)

### 3. Active Monitoring
- For subthreshold or mild depression: arrange follow-up within 2 weeks
- Discuss triggers, coping strategies, and lifestyle factors (sleep, exercise, substance use)
- Provide written information about depression and self-help resources
- Do not delay active treatment if symptoms worsen or the patient prefers intervention

### 4. Measurement-Based Care
- Use PHQ-9 at every clinical contact during active treatment
- Track progress against defined response and remission targets
- Use deterioration alerts (score increase ≥5 points) to trigger clinical review

## First-Line Treatment Recommendations (NG222 Updates)

NG222 significantly expanded the first-line options for less severe depression:

### For a New Episode of Less Severe Depression
The following are all considered first-line (patient preference guides choice):
- **Guided self-help** based on CBT principles (6-8 sessions, supported by a trained practitioner)
- **Group CBT** (typically 8-12 sessions)
- **Group behavioral activation** (typically 8-12 sessions)
- **Individual CBT** (6-8 sessions of low-intensity CBT, or full course if indicated)
- **Group exercise programs** (structured, supervised; new in NG222)
- **Group mindfulness/meditation** (new in NG222)
- **SSRIs** (if the patient prefers medication, or psychological interventions are declined/unavailable)

### For More Severe Depression
- **Individual CBT** (16-20 sessions) or **IPT** (16-20 sessions) — high-intensity psychotherapy
- **Antidepressant medication** (SSRI first-line; SNRI, mirtazapine as alternatives)
- **Combined treatment** (psychotherapy + medication) for the most severe presentations
- **Behavioral activation** (high-intensity, 16-20 sessions)
- **Couples therapy** (where relationship difficulties are a significant maintaining factor)

## Stepping Up and Stepping Down

### Criteria for Stepping Up
- Inadequate response after an adequate trial at the current step (typically 6-8 weeks)
- Worsening symptoms or emergence of suicidal ideation
- Significant functional deterioration
- Patient request for more intensive treatment
- New information suggesting greater severity than initially assessed

### Criteria for Stepping Down
- Sustained remission (PHQ-9 < 5 for 2+ consecutive assessments)
- Patient stable on continuation treatment
- Mutual agreement between patient and clinician
- Ensure adequate relapse prevention plan is in place before stepping down

## Safety and Risk Management

- Assess suicide risk at every contact, especially during transitions between steps
- Provide crisis contact information to all patients (Samaritans, NHS 111, local crisis teams)
- For patients prescribed medication: discuss onset lag (2-4 weeks), initial side effects, and importance of not stopping abruptly
- Monitor young adults (18-25) more closely in the first 4 weeks of antidepressant treatment
