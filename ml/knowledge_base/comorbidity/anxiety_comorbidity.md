# Anxiety and Depression Comorbidity

## Prevalence and Clinical Significance

- Over 50% of patients with MDD have a comorbid anxiety disorder (Kessler et al., 2005)
- The most common comorbid anxiety disorders: Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD)
- Comorbid anxiety predicts worse depression outcomes: slower response to treatment, lower remission rates, higher relapse risk, greater functional impairment, and increased suicide risk
- Anxiety often precedes depression developmentally — childhood/adolescent anxiety is a strong risk factor for adult MDD

## DSM-5 Anxious Distress Specifier

DSM-5 introduced a severity specifier for MDD with anxious distress. At least 2 of the following during the majority of days:
1. Feeling keyed up or tense
2. Feeling unusually restless
3. Difficulty concentrating because of worry
4. Fear that something awful may happen
5. Feeling that the individual might lose control

**Severity**: Mild (2 symptoms), Moderate (3), Moderate-Severe (4-5), Severe (4-5 with motor agitation)

Clinical relevance: Anxious depression is associated with poorer antidepressant response, longer time to remission, and higher suicide risk. Identifying this specifier changes treatment planning.

## Common Comorbid Anxiety Disorders

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
- Excessive, uncontrollable worry about multiple life domains for ≥6 months
- Overlapping symptoms with depression: fatigue, poor concentration, sleep disturbance, irritability
- Key differentiator: GAD is driven by pervasive worry; depression by low mood and anhedonia

### Panic Disorder
- Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks with physical symptoms (palpitations, shortness of breath, dizziness, chest pain)
- Anticipatory anxiety and avoidance behavior may mimic depressive withdrawal
- Comorbid panic + depression increases functional impairment significantly

### Social Anxiety Disorder
- Persistent fear of social/performance situations; avoidance mimics depressive social withdrawal
- Key differentiator: social anxiety is driven by fear of negative evaluation; depressive withdrawal by loss of interest or energy

## Screening: Use GAD-7 Alongside PHQ-9

The GAD-7 is a 7-item self-report measure validated for screening anxiety:
| GAD-7 Score | Severity |
|-------------|----------|
| 0-4 | Minimal |
| 5-9 | Mild |
| 10-14 | Moderate |
| 15-21 | Severe |

- Administer GAD-7 at the same time as PHQ-9 to capture comorbid anxiety
- Sensitivity 89%, specificity 82% for GAD at cutoff ≥10
- Also screens for panic disorder, social anxiety, and PTSD (though less specific for these)
- Track both scores over time — anxiety may improve at a different rate than depression

## Treatment Implications

### Pharmacotherapy
- **SSRIs and SNRIs treat both conditions**: Sertraline, escitalopram, venlafaxine, and duloxetine are all effective for MDD and most anxiety disorders
- Start at lower doses in anxious patients (SSRIs can transiently increase anxiety in the first 1-2 weeks)
- Benzodiazepines: Short-term relief of severe anxiety symptoms during SSRI initiation (2-4 weeks max); avoid long-term use due to dependence, cognitive effects, and fall risk
- Buspirone: Augmentation option for residual anxiety; no dependence risk; slow onset (2-4 weeks)
- Pregabalin: Second-line for GAD in some guidelines (NICE); not FDA-approved for anxiety in the US

### Psychotherapy
- **CBT addresses both conditions**: Cognitive restructuring targets both depressive and anxious cognitions; behavioral experiments address avoidance
- **Transdiagnostic CBT protocols** (Unified Protocol, Barlow) treat depression and anxiety simultaneously without separate protocols
- Behavioral activation for depression + gradual exposure for anxiety avoidance = complementary
- Mindfulness-based approaches reduce both rumination (depression) and worry (anxiety)

### Treatment Sequencing
- Treat both simultaneously — addressing only depression while ignoring comorbid anxiety leads to partial response
- If one disorder is clearly primary and maintaining the other, prioritize that disorder
- Monitor both PHQ-9 and GAD-7 throughout treatment to track differential response

## What to Tell Patients

- "Depression and anxiety frequently occur together — you are not unusual."
- "The same treatments (medication and therapy) are effective for both conditions."
- "Anxiety may temporarily increase when starting an antidepressant — this is normal and typically resolves within 1-2 weeks."
- "We will track both your depression and anxiety scores to make sure both are improving."
