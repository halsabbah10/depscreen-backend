## Safety Planning

A safety plan is a personalized, step-by-step guide created in advance to help you stay safe during moments of suicidal crisis. Developed by Stanley and Brown, safety planning is an evidence-based intervention recommended by the VA, NIMH, and leading clinical guidelines. It is one of the most important tools for managing suicidal thoughts.

**If you are in immediate danger, please call 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911 / your local emergency number now.**

### The Six Steps of a Safety Plan

**Step 1: Recognize your warning signs.**
Identify the thoughts, feelings, situations, or behaviors that signal a crisis may be building. Examples: "When I start thinking I am a burden," "When I isolate for more than a day," "When I stop eating."

**Step 2: Use internal coping strategies.**
List things you can do on your own to take your mind off distressing thoughts, without contacting another person. Examples: physical activity, grounding exercises (see grounding_techniques.md), journaling, taking a shower, going for a walk.

**Step 3: Reach out to people or social settings that provide distraction.**
Identify people you can contact or places you can go to help shift your focus. You do not need to disclose what you are feeling; the goal is connection. Examples: calling a friend, going to a coffee shop, visiting a family member.

**Step 4: Contact people you trust for help.**
List specific people you can tell that you are struggling. Include their names and phone numbers. These are people who will listen without judgment.

**Step 5: Contact professionals and crisis services.**
- **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text **988** (available 24/7 in the US)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **Veterans Crisis Line:** Call **988**, then press **1**
- **International Association for Suicide Prevention:** https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
- Your therapist's name and number: _______________
- Your psychiatrist's name and number: _______________
- Nearest emergency room: _______________

**Step 6: Make your environment safe.**
Work with a trusted person to reduce access to lethal means. This may include removing or securing firearms, medications, or other items. Means restriction is one of the most effective suicide prevention strategies.

### Important Reminders

- Create your safety plan **before** a crisis, when you are thinking clearly.
- Keep it accessible: on your phone, on paper in your wallet, or posted at home.
- Share it with someone you trust.
- Review and update it regularly with your therapist or counselor.
- Using your safety plan is a sign of strength, not weakness.

**You matter. You deserve support. Help is available right now.**
