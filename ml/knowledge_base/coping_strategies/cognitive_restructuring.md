## Cognitive Restructuring

Cognitive restructuring is a core technique from cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) that helps people identify, challenge, and reframe distorted or unhelpful thought patterns. It is particularly effective for managing feelings of worthlessness, excessive guilt, and the negative thinking patterns that accompany depression.

### How It Works

Depression often distorts thinking in predictable ways. Common cognitive distortions include:

- **All-or-nothing thinking:** "If I am not perfect, I am a total failure."
- **Overgeneralization:** "One thing went wrong, so everything always goes wrong."
- **Mental filtering:** Focusing only on negatives and ignoring positives.
- **Personalization:** Blaming yourself for events outside your control.
- **Should statements:** "I should be able to handle this. Something is wrong with me."
- **Fortune telling:** Assuming the worst outcome is inevitable.

Cognitive restructuring does not mean forcing yourself to "think positive." Instead, it means learning to evaluate your thoughts with the same fairness you would offer a friend.

### Practical Steps

1. **Catch the thought.** When you notice a shift in mood, pause and ask: "What was I just thinking?" Write it down.
2. **Identify the distortion.** Which pattern does this thought follow? Labeling it can reduce its power.
3. **Examine the evidence.** What facts support this thought? What facts contradict it? Would you say this to a friend in the same situation?
4. **Generate a balanced alternative.** Create a thought that accounts for the full picture. For example, instead of "I am useless," try "I am struggling right now, and that does not define my worth."
5. **Notice the effect.** After reframing, check in with your mood. Even a small shift matters.

### Building the Skill

Cognitive restructuring takes practice. Consider keeping a thought record or journal where you regularly work through these steps. Over time, balanced thinking becomes more automatic. Many people find that working with a therapist trained in CBT accelerates this process.
