## Grounding Techniques

Grounding techniques are evidence-based strategies from dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) and mindfulness-based approaches that help bring your attention back to the present moment. They are especially useful for managing psychomotor agitation, restlessness, anxiety, and the feeling of being overwhelmed that can accompany depression.

### How Grounding Works

When agitation or anxiety escalates, the body's stress response activates. Thoughts may race, muscles tense, and it becomes hard to think clearly. Grounding interrupts this cycle by redirecting focus to your immediate sensory experience, anchoring you in the here and now rather than the spiral of distressing thoughts.

### Sensory Grounding Techniques

**The 5-4-3-2-1 technique:**
- Name **5** things you can see
- Name **4** things you can touch
- Name **3** things you can hear
- Name **2** things you can smell
- Name **1** thing you can taste

This engages all five senses and shifts attention away from internal distress.

**Other sensory strategies:**
- Hold an ice cube or splash cold water on your face (activates the dive reflex, slowing heart rate)
- Press your feet firmly into the floor and notice the sensation of contact
- Hold a textured object (a smooth stone, a piece of fabric) and focus on how it feels
- Listen to a specific sound in your environment and follow it closely

### Breath-Based Grounding

- **Box breathing:** Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4 counts, exhale for 4 counts, hold for 4 counts. Repeat 4 times.
- **Extended exhale:** Inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 6 to 8 counts. Longer exhales activate the parasympathetic nervous system.

### Movement-Based Grounding

- Progressive muscle relaxation: Tense and release each muscle group from toes to head.
- Slow, deliberate walking while noticing each step.
- Gentle stretching with full attention on the body's sensations.

### When to Practice

Grounding is most effective when practiced regularly, not just during moments of crisis. Building familiarity with these techniques during calm moments makes them more accessible when agitation peaks.
