## Nutrition Guidance for Depression-Related Appetite Changes

Changes in appetite are a common feature of depression, whether eating significantly more or less than usual. While nutrition alone is not a treatment for depression, maintaining adequate and balanced nutrition supports brain function, energy levels, and overall recovery.

### If Your Appetite Has Decreased

- **Eat small amounts frequently.** Instead of three large meals, aim for five to six smaller ones. Even a few bites count.
- **Keep easy foods accessible.** Stock items that require little or no preparation: fruit, nuts, yogurt, cheese, crackers, or pre-made smoothies.
- **Set gentle reminders.** Use phone alarms or mealtime cues (such as eating when a certain show comes on) to prompt eating, since hunger signals may be muted.
- **Focus on nutrient-dense options.** When appetite is low, make each bite count with foods rich in protein, healthy fats, and vitamins.
- **Drink calories if eating feels difficult.** Smoothies, soups, and nutritional supplement drinks can provide nourishment when solid food is unappealing.

### If Your Appetite Has Increased

- **Practice mindful eating.** Before eating, pause and check in: "Am I physically hungry, or am I eating to manage an emotion?" Both are valid, but awareness helps.
- **Keep structured meal times.** Regular meals help prevent the extreme hunger that leads to overeating.
- **Include protein and fiber.** These nutrients promote satiety and steady blood sugar, reducing cravings.
- **Reduce access to trigger foods.** This is not about restriction but about making the default option a nourishing one.
- **Treat yourself with compassion.** Using food for comfort is a human response to distress. Shame only worsens the cycle.

### Foods That Support Mental Health

Emerging research in nutritional psychiatry suggests that the following support brain health:

- Omega-3 fatty acids (fatty fish, walnuts, flaxseed)
- Whole grains and complex carbohydrates
- Leafy greens and colorful vegetables
- Fermented foods that support gut health (yogurt, kimchi)
- Adequate hydration

This information is for educational purposes and does not replace professional treatment or nutritional counseling.
