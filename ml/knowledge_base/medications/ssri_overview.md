# Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs)

## Mechanism of Action

SSRIs selectively block the reuptake of serotonin (5-HT) at the presynaptic neuron, increasing serotonin availability in the synaptic cleft. This selective action on the serotonin transporter (SERT) distinguishes SSRIs from older antidepressants, resulting in a more favorable side effect profile. Downstream neuroadaptive changes — including receptor desensitization and enhanced serotonergic neurotransmission — are believed to mediate the therapeutic effect over 2-6 weeks.

## Clinical Considerations

- **First-line treatment** for major depressive disorder per APA, NICE, and CANMAT guidelines.
- Escitalopram and sertraline are frequently preferred as initial choices due to favorable tolerability and fewer drug interactions.
- Fluoxetine has the longest half-life (~4-6 days for active metabolite norfluoxetine), making it the most forgiving for missed doses and the least likely to cause discontinuation syndrome.
- Paroxetine has the highest anticholinergic activity among SSRIs and the shortest half-life, making discontinuation syndrome more likely.
- Fluvoxamine is primarily used for obsessive-compulsive disorder but has antidepressant efficacy.

## Common Side Effects

- **Gastrointestinal**: Nausea (most common early side effect), diarrhea, constipation. Typically resolves within 1-2 weeks.
- **Central nervous system**: Headache, insomnia or somnolence, dizziness, activation/anxiety (especially early in treatment).
- **Sexual dysfunction**: Decreased libido, anorgasmia, delayed ejaculation. Affects 25-73% of patients. Often persistent and a leading cause of non-adherence.
- **Weight changes**: Initial weight loss possible; long-term weight gain (particularly paroxetine, >6 months).
- **Other**: Excessive sweating, dry mouth, tremor, bruising.

## Serious Adverse Effects

- **QT prolongation**: Citalopram carries a dose-dependent risk of QTc prolongation (FDA-mandated max 40 mg/day). Escitalopram has a lesser but still clinically relevant risk above 20 mg/day. Obtain baseline ECG in patients with cardiac risk factors.
- **Serotonin syndrome**: Rare but life-threatening. Risk increases with concomitant serotonergic agents (MAOIs, triptans, tramadol, St. John's Wort). Triad: altered mental status, autonomic instability, neuromuscular hyperactivity.
- **Hyponatremia (SIADH)**: More common in elderly patients. Monitor sodium in at-risk populations.
- **Bleeding risk**: SSRIs impair platelet aggregation via serotonin depletion in platelets. Increased risk of GI bleeding, especially with concurrent NSAIDs or anticoagulants.

## FDA Black Box Warning

All SSRIs carry a black box warning for increased risk of suicidal thinking and behavior in children, adolescents, and young adults (under age 25) during initial treatment or dose changes. Close monitoring is essential during the first 1-2 months and after dose adjustments. This risk must be weighed against the substantial risk of untreated depression.

## When SSRIs Are Preferred

- First depressive episode, moderate to severe.
- Comorbid anxiety disorders (GAD, panic disorder, social anxiety, OCD).
- Patients prioritizing a well-established safety profile.
- Elderly patients (escitalopram, sertraline preferred).
- Patients where drug interaction risk must be minimized (escitalopram, sertraline have fewer CYP interactions).

## When to Consider Alternatives

- Treatment-resistant depression after adequate SSRI trial (≥6-8 weeks at therapeutic dose).
- Intolerable sexual dysfunction (consider bupropion or mirtazapine).
- Significant fatigue or pain component (consider SNRIs).
- Bipolar depression (SSRIs may induce mania; mood stabilizer required first).

## Key Patient Counseling Points

- Therapeutic effects typically take 2-4 weeks to emerge; full benefit may take 6-8 weeks.
- Side effects (nausea, headache, anxiety) are often worst in the first 1-2 weeks and tend to improve.
- Do not stop abruptly — taper under medical supervision to avoid discontinuation syndrome.
- Report any worsening mood, agitation, or suicidal thoughts immediately.
