# Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs)

## Mechanism of Action

SNRIs inhibit the reuptake of both serotonin (5-HT) and norepinephrine (NE), increasing the availability of both neurotransmitters in the synaptic cleft. The dual mechanism provides broader neurochemical coverage compared to SSRIs. Norepinephrine reuptake inhibition contributes to effects on energy, motivation, concentration, and pain modulation. At lower doses, serotonergic effects predominate; noradrenergic effects become more prominent at higher doses (particularly with venlafaxine).

## SNRI Medications and Dose Ranges

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Duloxetine | Cymbalta | 30 mg/day | 60-120 mg/day | 120 mg/day |

## Clinical Considerations

- **Venlafaxine**: Most studied SNRI. At doses <150 mg, it functions primarily as an SSRI. Noradrenergic effects meaningful at ≥150 mg. Some meta-analyses suggest slightly higher remission rates compared to SSRIs, though clinical significance is debated. Available in immediate-release (IR) and extended-release (XR) formulations; XR strongly preferred for tolerability.
- **Duloxetine**: FDA-approved for MDD, GAD, diabetic neuropathy, fibromyalgia, and chronic musculoskeletal pain. Strong evidence for comorbid depression and pain conditions. Avoid in hepatic impairment and heavy alcohol use.
- **Desvenlafaxine**: Active metabolite of venlafaxine. Fixed dosing (50 mg) simplifies titration. Fewer CYP2D6 interactions than venlafaxine. Minimal dose-response advantage above 50 mg.

## Preferred When

- Depression with prominent **fatigue, low energy, or psychomotor retardation** (noradrenergic component).
- Comorbid **chronic pain** conditions (neuropathic pain, fibromyalgia, musculoskeletal pain).
- Comorbid **generalized anxiety disorder** (duloxetine, venlafaxine both FDA-approved for GAD).
- Inadequate response to SSRI monotherapy.
- Depression with significant **cognitive complaints** (attention, concentration).

## Common Side Effects

- All SSRI-class side effects apply: nausea, headache, insomnia, sexual dysfunction, sweating.
- **Elevated blood pressure**: Dose-dependent, more pronounced with venlafaxine (5-13% incidence at higher doses). Monitor BP at baseline and regularly during treatment. May be clinically significant in patients with pre-existing hypertension.
- **Excessive sweating**: More common than with SSRIs.
- **Urinary hesitancy**: Noradrenergic effect; more frequent with higher doses.
- **Constipation**: More common than diarrhea (contrast with SSRIs).

## Serious Adverse Effects

- **Hypertensive crisis**: Rare, but sustained BP elevation possible, especially venlafaxine >225 mg. Contraindicated in uncontrolled hypertension.
- **Hepatotoxicity**: Duloxetine — rare cases of severe liver injury. Avoid in substantial alcohol use or pre-existing liver disease.
- **Serotonin syndrome**: Same risk profile as SSRIs. Never combine with MAOIs (14-day washout required).
- **Discontinuation syndrome**: Particularly severe with venlafaxine due to short half-life (~5 hours). Symptoms include dizziness, nausea, paresthesias ("brain zaps"), irritability. Gradual taper essential.
- **Suicidality**: Same FDA black box warning as SSRIs for patients under 25.

## Dosing Guidance

- **Venlafaxine**: Start 37.5-75 mg/day, increase by 75 mg every 4-7 days. Most patients require 150-225 mg/day for full dual-mechanism benefit. XR formulation taken once daily.
- **Duloxetine**: Start 30 mg/day for 1 week, then 60 mg/day. Some patients benefit from 90-120 mg/day. Do not crush or chew enteric-coated capsules (destroyed by gastric acid).
- **Desvenlafaxine**: Start and maintain at 50 mg/day. Dose increases to 100 mg show limited additional benefit with more side effects.

## Comparison with SSRIs

| Feature | SSRIs | SNRIs |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Serotonin only | Serotonin + norepinephrine |
| Pain efficacy | Limited | Moderate-strong (duloxetine) |
| BP effects | Minimal | Dose-dependent elevation |
| Discontinuation | Moderate (except fluoxetine) | Severe (especially venlafaxine) |
| Energy/fatigue | Variable | Often improves |
| CYP interactions | Variable by agent | Generally fewer (desvenlafaxine) |

## Key Patient Counseling Points

- Blood pressure should be checked before starting and monitored regularly.
- Report persistent headaches, chest pain, or rapid heartbeat.
- Therapeutic onset 2-4 weeks; full benefit 6-8 weeks.
- Never stop abruptly — venlafaxine discontinuation syndrome can be severe even after missing a single dose.
- Duloxetine capsules must be swallowed whole.
