# Augmentation Strategies for Depression

## When Augmentation Is Considered

Augmentation means adding a second medication to an antidepressant that has produced a partial response. It is preferred over switching when:
- The patient has achieved some benefit (≥25-50% symptom reduction) on the current antidepressant.
- Side effects are tolerable.
- The patient has been on an adequate dose for adequate duration (≥6-8 weeks).
- Complete medication change risks losing partial gains.

## Lithium Augmentation

### Evidence Level: Strongest

- **The most extensively studied augmentation strategy in depression.** Over 30 randomized controlled trials, multiple meta-analyses.
- Effective regardless of which antidepressant class is being augmented (SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs).
- NNT (number needed to treat) of approximately 5 — one of the strongest effect sizes in psychiatry.
- Response typically seen within 2-4 weeks.

### Dosing
- Target serum level: 0.6-0.8 mEq/L for augmentation (lower than bipolar mania dosing).
- Typical dose: 600-900 mg/day (adjusted by levels).
- Start at 300 mg/day, titrate based on serum levels.

### Monitoring
- Serum lithium levels: 5-7 days after dose change, then every 3-6 months at steady state.
- Baseline and periodic: renal function (creatinine, eGFR), thyroid function (TSH), calcium, ECG.
- Signs of toxicity (>1.5 mEq/L): tremor, nausea, diarrhea, confusion, ataxia. Level >2.0 mEq/L is a medical emergency.

### Practical Considerations
- Narrow therapeutic index — requires regular blood monitoring.
- Dehydration, NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, and diuretics can elevate lithium levels.
- Hypothyroidism and nephrogenic diabetes insipidus with long-term use.
- Despite the monitoring burden, lithium remains the gold standard augmentation agent.
- **Unique anti-suicidal properties** — the only medication with strong evidence for reducing suicide risk independently of mood stabilization.

## Atypical Antipsychotic Augmentation

### Aripiprazole (Abilify)

- **FDA-approved for adjunctive treatment of MDD** (2007).
- Dose: 2-15 mg/day (typically 2-5 mg for depression augmentation — lower than schizophrenia dosing).
- Partial D2 agonist + 5-HT1A partial agonist + 5-HT2A antagonist.
- Weight gain less than other atypical antipsychotics but still present.
- Akathisia (restlessness) is the most common limiting side effect (~12%).
- Monitor: weight, metabolic panel, fasting glucose/lipids at baseline and periodically.

### Quetiapine (Seroquel XR)

- **FDA-approved for adjunctive treatment of MDD** (extended-release formulation only).
- Dose: 50-300 mg/day for depression augmentation.
- Strong sedating properties — useful for insomnia and anxious depression.
- Side effects: sedation, weight gain, metabolic syndrome, orthostatic hypotension.
- Higher metabolic risk than aripiprazole.
- Monitor: weight, fasting glucose, lipid panel, blood pressure.

### Brexpiprazole (Rexulti)

- **FDA-approved for adjunctive treatment of MDD** (2015).
- Dose: 0.5-3 mg/day.
- Similar mechanism to aripiprazole but lower D2 affinity.
- May have lower akathisia rates than aripiprazole.
- Weight gain and metabolic monitoring required.

### Cariprazine (Vraylar)

- **FDA-approved for adjunctive treatment of MDD** (2022).
- Dose: 1.5 mg/day for depression augmentation.
- Unique D3-preferring partial agonist.
- Long half-life metabolites (weeks).

## Thyroid Hormone (T3) Augmentation

### Evidence
- Liothyronine (T3, Cytomel) 25-50 mcg/day added to TCAs or SSRIs.
- Multiple trials show benefit, particularly with TCAs. Evidence with SSRIs is more limited.
- May be more effective in women.
- Mechanism unclear — may enhance serotonergic neurotransmission.

### Dosing
- Start 25 mcg/day, increase to 50 mcg/day after 1-2 weeks if tolerated.
- T3 preferred over T4 (levothyroxine) for augmentation — T3 showed benefit in STAR*D; T4 did not.

### Monitoring
- TSH, free T4 at baseline and periodically.
- Generally well-tolerated at augmentation doses.
- Caution in cardiac disease (can increase heart rate, rarely arrhythmias).
- Monitor bone density with long-term use.

## Combination Antidepressant Strategies

### SSRI/SNRI + Bupropion
- **Most commonly used combination in practice.**
- Complementary mechanisms: serotonin/norepinephrine (SSRI/SNRI) + norepinephrine/dopamine (bupropion).
- May offset SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction and fatigue.
- Generally well-tolerated. Bupropion inhibits CYP2D6 — consider interaction with primary antidepressant.
- STAR*D Level 3 supported this combination.

### SSRI + Mirtazapine ("California Rocket Fuel")
- Complementary mechanisms: SERT blockade (SSRI) + NaSSA (mirtazapine).
- Mirtazapine addresses insomnia, poor appetite, and provides anxiolytic effects.
- Concern: additive weight gain, excessive sedation.
- Reserve for more difficult-to-treat cases.

### SSRI + Low-Dose TCA
- Occasionally used (e.g., SSRI + nortriptyline for pain comorbidity).
- **Caution**: SSRIs that inhibit CYP2D6 (fluoxetine, paroxetine) can dramatically increase TCA levels. Monitor TCA serum levels and ECG.

## Other Augmentation Approaches

### Psychostimulants
- Methylphenidate or modafinil for residual fatigue, apathy, cognitive impairment.
- Limited controlled evidence for depression augmentation. Used off-label.
- Modafinil (100-200 mg/day): better evidence for fatigue and sleepiness than core depressive symptoms.
- Risk: insomnia, anxiety, appetite suppression, misuse potential (methylphenidate).

### S-Adenosyl-Methionine (SAMe)
- Some RCT evidence as augmentation to SSRIs.
- Dose: 800-1600 mg/day.
- Generally well-tolerated. Theoretical serotonin syndrome risk.
- More evidence needed before routine recommendation.

### L-Methylfolate
- Deplin (15 mg/day): medical food indicated for depression.
- May benefit patients with MTHFR polymorphisms or folate deficiency.
- Low risk profile. Some RCT evidence supporting SSRI augmentation.

## Choosing an Augmentation Strategy

| Agent | Evidence Strength | Monitoring Burden | Key Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lithium | Strongest | High (blood levels, renal, thyroid) | Anti-suicidal, proven efficacy |
| Aripiprazole | Strong (FDA-approved) | Moderate (metabolic) | Well-tolerated at low dose |
| Quetiapine XR | Strong (FDA-approved) | Moderate (metabolic) | Insomnia, anxiety benefit |
| T3 | Moderate | Low | Simple, well-tolerated |
| Bupropion add-on | Moderate (STAR*D) | Low | Offsets sexual side effects |
| Mirtazapine add-on | Moderate | Low | Insomnia, appetite benefit |
