# Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs) and MAOIs

## Historical Context

TCAs (introduced 1950s) and MAOIs (introduced late 1950s) were the first effective pharmacological treatments for depression. While largely supplanted by SSRIs and SNRIs as first-line agents due to safety and tolerability, they remain important options for treatment-resistant depression and specific clinical scenarios where newer agents have failed.

## Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)

### Mechanism of Action
TCAs inhibit the reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine with varying selectivity. They also block muscarinic (M1), histaminergic (H1), and alpha-1 adrenergic receptors, which accounts for their broad side effect profile.

### Common TCAs and Dose Ranges

| Medication | Brand Name | Therapeutic Range | Properties |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amitriptyline | Elavil | 75-200 mg/day | Most sedating; strong anticholinergic |
| Nortriptyline | Pamelor | 50-150 mg/day | Less sedating; therapeutic window (50-150 ng/mL) |
| Imipramine | Tofranil | 75-200 mg/day | Balanced NE/5-HT reuptake |
| Desipramine | Norpramin | 75-200 mg/day | Most noradrenergic; least sedating |
| Clomipramine | Anafranil | 75-250 mg/day | Most serotonergic TCA; gold standard for OCD |
| Doxepin | Sinequan | 75-300 mg/day | Potent H1 blocker; low-dose for insomnia (3-6 mg) |

### When TCAs Are Still Used
- **Treatment-resistant depression**: After failure of 2+ adequate trials of newer agents.
- **Neuropathic pain**: Amitriptyline, nortriptyline first-line for diabetic neuropathy, postherpetic neuralgia.
- **Migraine prophylaxis**: Amitriptyline well-established.
- **OCD**: Clomipramine remains highly effective, sometimes superior to SSRIs.
- **Insomnia**: Low-dose doxepin (3-6 mg) is FDA-approved for sleep maintenance.

### Side Effects (The "Dirty Drug" Profile)
- **Anticholinergic**: Dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, blurred vision, cognitive impairment. Worse with amitriptyline, doxepin, clomipramine.
- **Histaminergic (H1)**: Sedation, weight gain.
- **Alpha-1 adrenergic**: Orthostatic hypotension, dizziness, reflex tachycardia. Fall risk in elderly.
- **Cardiac**: QRS and QTc prolongation, arrhythmias. ECG recommended before initiation and at therapeutic doses.

### Toxicity in Overdose
TCAs are **among the most lethal drugs in overdose**. As few as 10-day supply can be fatal. Toxicity manifests as:
- Cardiac arrhythmias (widened QRS → ventricular tachycardia)
- Seizures
- Anticholinergic delirium
- Hypotension

This lethality is a primary reason TCAs are not first-line, particularly in patients with suicidal ideation.

### Monitoring
- **Baseline ECG** before starting; repeat at therapeutic doses.
- **Therapeutic drug monitoring**: Nortriptyline has a well-defined therapeutic window (50-150 ng/mL). Levels for other TCAs guide toxicity avoidance.
- Prescribe limited quantities in patients with suicide risk.

## Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs)

### Mechanism of Action
MAOIs irreversibly (or reversibly, in the case of moclobemide) inhibit monoamine oxidase enzymes:
- **MAO-A**: Metabolizes serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine.
- **MAO-B**: Metabolizes dopamine, phenylethylamine.
Non-selective MAOIs inhibit both isoforms, broadly increasing monoamine availability.

### MAOI Medications

| Medication | Brand Name | Type | Dose Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phenelzine | Nardil | Non-selective, irreversible | 45-90 mg/day |
| Tranylcypromine | Parnate | Non-selective, irreversible | 30-60 mg/day |
| Selegiline patch | EMSAM | MAO-B selective (low dose) | 6-12 mg/24h patch |
| Moclobemide | Manerix | MAO-A selective, reversible | 300-600 mg/day |

*Moclobemide not available in the US. Selegiline at 6 mg/day patch does not require dietary restrictions.*

### When MAOIs Are Still Used
- **Atypical depression** (mood reactivity, leaden paralysis, rejection sensitivity, hypersomnia, hyperphagia) — MAOIs show superior efficacy in this subtype.
- **Treatment-resistant depression**: After multiple adequate trials of other classes have failed.
- **Anergic, amotivational presentations**: Tranylcypromine has mild amphetamine-like stimulant properties.

### The Tyramine ("Cheese") Reaction
MAOIs inhibit gut MAO-A, which normally metabolizes dietary tyramine. Unmetabolized tyramine triggers massive norepinephrine release, causing **hypertensive crisis**:
- Severe headache, neck stiffness, nausea, diaphoresis
- Hypertensive emergency (BP >180/120) → risk of stroke, death

**Dietary restrictions** (oral MAOIs at standard doses):
- Aged cheeses, cured meats, fermented foods (sauerkraut, soy sauce, miso)
- Draft/tap beer (bottled is generally safe in moderation)
- Overripe bananas, broad beans
- Concentrated yeast extracts (Marmite)

### Drug Interactions — Critical
- **Serotonergic drugs**: Absolute contraindication with SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs (clomipramine), meperidine, dextromethorphan, tramadol, St. John's Wort. **14-day washout** required before or after MAOI (5 weeks for fluoxetine due to long half-life).
- **Sympathomimetics**: Pseudoephedrine, phenylephrine — hypertensive crisis risk.
- **Anesthetic considerations**: Inform all healthcare providers, especially before surgery.

### Selegiline Transdermal Patch (EMSAM)
- Bypasses gut absorption, reducing tyramine interaction risk.
- At 6 mg/24h: **no dietary restrictions required** (FDA-approved without them).
- At 9 mg and 12 mg/24h: dietary restrictions apply.
- Application site reactions are the most common side effect.
- Practical advantage: improved adherence for patients who cannot manage dietary restrictions.

## Comparison Summary

| Feature | TCAs | MAOIs |
|---|---|---|
| Efficacy for TRD | Good | Excellent (atypical depression) |
| Side effect burden | High (anticholinergic, cardiac) | Moderate + dietary restrictions |
| Overdose lethality | Very high | Moderate-high |
| Drug interactions | Moderate | Extensive and dangerous |
| Monitoring required | ECG, drug levels | BP, dietary compliance |
| Modern role | Pain, insomnia, OCD, TRD | Atypical depression, TRD |

## Key Points for Patients
- These medications are prescribed when newer antidepressants have not worked well enough.
- TCAs: Do not take extra doses. Keep medications locked and away from others. Report dizziness on standing, rapid heartbeat, or difficulty urinating.
- MAOIs: Carry the dietary restriction card at all times. Inform every doctor, dentist, and pharmacist. Go to the ER for sudden severe headache with neck stiffness.
