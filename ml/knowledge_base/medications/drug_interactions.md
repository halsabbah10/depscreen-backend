# Antidepressant Drug Interactions

## Serotonin Syndrome

### What It Is
A potentially life-threatening condition caused by excessive serotonergic activity in the central nervous system. Results from combining two or more serotonergic agents or overdose of a single agent.

### Clinical Presentation (Hunter Criteria)
Diagnosis requires a serotonergic agent PLUS any of:
- Spontaneous clonus
- Inducible clonus + agitation or diaphoresis
- Ocular clonus + agitation or diaphoresis
- Tremor + hyperreflexia
- Hypertonia + temperature >38°C + ocular or inducible clonus

**Triad**: Altered mental status, autonomic instability, neuromuscular hyperactivity.

### High-Risk Combinations

| Antidepressant | Dangerous Combination | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| Any SSRI/SNRI | MAOI (any type) | **Absolute contraindication** |
| Any SSRI/SNRI | Another SSRI/SNRI | High risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Tramadol | High risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Meperidine (Demerol) | High risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Triptans (sumatriptan, etc.) | Moderate risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Dextromethorphan (cough syrup) | Moderate risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | St. John's Wort | Moderate risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Lithium | Low-moderate risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Linezolid (antibiotic — weak MAOI) | High risk |
| Any serotonergic AD | Methylene blue (weak MAOI) | High risk |

### Washout Periods for MAOI Transitions
- SSRI/SNRI → MAOI: **14 days** minimum (5 weeks for fluoxetine due to norfluoxetine half-life).
- MAOI → SSRI/SNRI: **14 days** minimum (irreversible MAOIs — enzyme regeneration time).
- These washout periods are non-negotiable.

## CYP450 Interactions

Antidepressants are metabolized by and may inhibit cytochrome P450 enzymes, affecting the levels of co-administered drugs.

### CYP2D6 Inhibitors (Most Clinically Significant)

| Strong Inhibitors | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Fluoxetine** | Potent CYP2D6 inhibitor — persists for weeks after discontinuation (long half-life) |
| **Paroxetine** | Potent CYP2D6 inhibitor |
| **Bupropion** | Moderate-strong CYP2D6 inhibitor |

**Affected medications** (CYP2D6 substrates — levels may increase):
- Codeine → morphine conversion **blocked** (reduced efficacy)
- Tamoxifen → endoxifen conversion **blocked** (critical — **avoid fluoxetine/paroxetine in breast cancer patients on tamoxifen**)
- Metoprolol, carvedilol (increased levels → bradycardia, hypotension)
- Aripiprazole, risperidone (increased levels → extrapyramidal effects)
- Atomoxetine (increased levels → cardiac effects)
- Vortioxetine (reduce dose by 50% with CYP2D6 inhibitors)

### CYP3A4 Interactions

| Antidepressant | Effect on CYP3A4 |
|---|---|
| Fluvoxamine | **Strong inhibitor** — most CYP3A4 interaction potential of any SSRI |
| Fluoxetine | Moderate inhibitor |
| Others | Minimal |

**Fluvoxamine caution**: Dramatically increases levels of CYP3A4 substrates including:
- Benzodiazepines (alprazolam, triazolam — avoid combination)
- Theophylline (toxic levels possible)
- Warfarin (increased anticoagulation)
- Caffeine (levels significantly elevated)

### CYP1A2 Interactions
- **Fluvoxamine**: Strong CYP1A2 inhibitor.
- Affects: Clozapine, theophylline, caffeine, tizanidine.
- Clinical significance: Fluvoxamine + clozapine can cause clozapine toxicity.

## Bleeding Risk

### Mechanism
SSRIs and SNRIs deplete platelet serotonin stores, impairing platelet aggregation. This effect is additive with other agents affecting hemostasis.

### High-Risk Combinations
- **SSRI/SNRI + NSAIDs**: 2-4x increased risk of upper GI bleeding. Consider PPI prophylaxis if both are necessary.
- **SSRI/SNRI + Aspirin**: Additive bleeding risk.
- **SSRI/SNRI + Anticoagulants** (warfarin, DOACs): Monitor INR more frequently with warfarin. Increased bleeding risk with all anticoagulants.
- **SSRI/SNRI + Antiplatelet agents** (clopidogrel): Additive effect.

### Clinical Guidance
- In patients requiring chronic NSAID use, consider antidepressants with lower serotonergic potency (bupropion, mirtazapine) or add gastroprotection.
- Perioperative: SSRIs/SNRIs do not typically need to be stopped before surgery, but inform the surgical team.

## Alcohol Interactions

- **Pharmacodynamic**: Alcohol is a CNS depressant. Combined with sedating antidepressants (mirtazapine, trazodone, TCAs), excessive sedation, impaired coordination, and respiratory depression possible.
- **Pharmacokinetic**: Chronic alcohol use induces CYP enzymes, potentially reducing antidepressant levels. Acute heavy intake inhibits CYP enzymes, potentially increasing levels.
- **Clinical**: Alcohol worsens depression, impairs judgment, and interferes with treatment adherence. Patients should limit or avoid alcohol.
- **Duloxetine**: Specifically contraindicated in heavy alcohol use due to hepatotoxicity risk.

## Additional Important Interactions

### QT Prolongation
- Citalopram + other QT-prolonging agents (antiarrhythmics, fluoroquinolones, antipsychotics, methadone) → additive QTc prolongation → risk of torsades de pointes.
- Escitalopram: lesser but still relevant risk.
- Obtain ECG if combining QT-prolonging medications.

### TCAs
- TCAs + anticholinergic agents → compounded constipation, urinary retention, cognitive impairment, delirium (especially in elderly).
- TCAs + CYP2D6 inhibitors (fluoxetine, paroxetine) → elevated TCA levels → cardiotoxicity risk. **Monitor TCA levels**.

### Bupropion
- Bupropion + medications lowering seizure threshold (antipsychotics, tramadol, theophylline, systemic corticosteroids) → increased seizure risk.
- Bupropion + alcohol/benzodiazepine withdrawal → seizure risk (contraindicated).

## Practical Guidance for Patients

1. **Always tell every healthcare provider** (doctors, dentists, pharmacists) about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
2. **St. John's Wort** is an antidepressant — do not combine with prescribed antidepressants.
3. **Check cough/cold medications** — many contain dextromethorphan, which interacts with serotonergic antidepressants.
4. **Avoid grapefruit juice** in large quantities if on medications metabolized by CYP3A4.
5. **Pain management**: Inform providers about your antidepressant before receiving any pain medication, especially tramadol or meperidine.
