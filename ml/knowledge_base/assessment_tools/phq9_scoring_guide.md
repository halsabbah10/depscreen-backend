# PHQ-9: Patient Health Questionnaire-9

## Overview

The PHQ-9 is a 9-item self-report measure that assesses the frequency of depressive symptoms over the past two weeks. Each item maps directly to a DSM-5 criterion for Major Depressive Disorder, making it both a severity measure and a diagnostic aid.

## Items and Response Options

Each item is scored 0-3:
- **0** = Not at all
- **1** = Several days
- **2** = More than half the days
- **3** = Nearly every day

| Item | Symptom Domain |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | Little interest or pleasure in doing things (anhedonia) |
| 2 | Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless (depressed mood) |
| 3 | Trouble falling/staying asleep, or sleeping too much |
| 4 | Feeling tired or having little energy |
| 5 | Poor appetite or overeating |
| 6 | Feeling bad about yourself, or that you are a failure |
| 7 | Trouble concentrating on things |
| 8 | Moving or speaking noticeably slowly or being fidgety/restless |
| 9 | Thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself |

## Severity Ranges and Clinical Actions

| Score Range | Severity | Clinical Action |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| 0-4 | Minimal/None | No treatment indicated; monitor if subclinical symptoms present |
| 5-9 | Mild | Watchful waiting; repeat PHQ-9 at follow-up; consider psychoeducation and self-help |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Treatment plan indicated: psychotherapy (CBT, IPT) and/or pharmacotherapy; patient preference guides choice |
| 15-19 | Moderately Severe | Active treatment strongly recommended: combined psychotherapy and pharmacotherapy preferred |
| 20-27 | Severe | Immediate treatment with pharmacotherapy and psychotherapy; assess for hospitalization; frequent monitoring |

## CRITICAL: Item 9 Safety Protocol

**ANY score greater than 0 on Item 9 (suicidal ideation) requires immediate risk assessment**, regardless of total score. A patient scoring "1" (several days) on Item 9 with a total score of 5 (mild) still requires a structured safety evaluation.

Actions for positive Item 9:
- Conduct a thorough suicide risk assessment (intent, plan, means, timeline, protective factors)
- Document the assessment and clinical decision-making
- Implement a safety plan if risk is identified
- Consider referral to crisis services if imminent risk is present
- Follow up within 24-48 hours for elevated risk

## Diagnostic and Psychometric Properties

- **Sensitivity**: 88% at cutoff score of 10 (for detecting MDD)
- **Specificity**: 88% at cutoff score of 10
- **Positive predictive value** varies by prevalence in the population screened
- **Internal consistency**: Cronbach's alpha 0.86-0.89 across studies
- **Test-retest reliability**: ICC = 0.84

## Treatment Response Definitions

| Metric | Definition | Clinical Meaning |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Response | ≥50% reduction from baseline score | Clinically meaningful improvement; continue current treatment |
| Partial response | 25-49% reduction from baseline | Some improvement; consider treatment optimization |
| Non-response | <25% reduction from baseline | Inadequate improvement; reassess diagnosis and treatment plan |
| Remission | Total score < 5 | Minimal or no depressive symptoms; treatment goal achieved |
| Reliable change | ≥6 point change from baseline | Change exceeds measurement error; statistically meaningful |

## Monitoring Schedule

- **Baseline**: Administer at initial assessment
- **Acute phase**: Every 2-4 weeks during active treatment
- **Continuation phase**: Monthly for 4-9 months after response
- **Maintenance phase**: Every 3 months for patients in long-term treatment

## Limitations

- Self-report measure subject to response bias
- 2-week recall window may miss fluctuating symptoms
- Does not assess all DSM-5 specifiers (e.g., anxious distress, mixed features)
- Screening tool, not a standalone diagnostic instrument; clinical interview required for diagnosis

**Sources**: Kroenke et al. (2001), APA Practice Guidelines (2010), AHRQ Screening for Depression (2016).
