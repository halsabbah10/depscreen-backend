# GAD-7: Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-Item Scale

## Overview

The GAD-7 is a 7-item self-report measure assessing the frequency of anxiety symptoms over the past two weeks. Originally developed to screen for Generalized Anxiety Disorder, it also has good sensitivity for detecting panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder, making it a useful transdiagnostic anxiety screener.

## Items and Response Options

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following:

Each item scored 0-3:
- **0** = Not at all
- **1** = Several days
- **2** = More than half the days
- **3** = Nearly every day

| Item | Symptom Domain |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge |
| 2 | Not being able to stop or control worrying |
| 3 | Worrying too much about different things |
| 4 | Trouble relaxing |
| 5 | Being so restless that it is hard to sit still |
| 6 | Becoming easily annoyed or irritable |
| 7 | Feeling afraid, as if something awful might happen |

**Total score range: 0-21**

## Severity Ranges and Clinical Actions

| Score Range | Severity | Clinical Action |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| 0-4 | Minimal | No intervention required; monitor if subclinical symptoms |
| 5-9 | Mild | Monitor symptoms; provide psychoeducation; reassess in 2-4 weeks |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Clinical evaluation indicated; consider CBT and/or pharmacotherapy |
| 15-21 | Severe | Active treatment strongly recommended; combined CBT and pharmacotherapy preferred |

## Psychometric Properties

- **Sensitivity**: 89% at cutoff score of 10 (for detecting GAD)
- **Specificity**: 82% at cutoff score of 10
- **Internal consistency**: Cronbach's alpha = 0.92
- **Test-retest reliability**: ICC = 0.83
- **Area under the ROC curve**: 0.91 (for any anxiety disorder at cutoff 8)

## Depression-Anxiety Comorbidity

More than 50% of patients with a depressive disorder also meet criteria for an anxiety disorder, and vice versa. This comorbidity has significant clinical implications:

- **Poorer outcomes**: Comorbid depression and anxiety predict slower treatment response and higher relapse rates
- **Higher severity**: Comorbid patients tend to have more severe functional impairment
- **Suicide risk**: Anxiety comorbidity increases suicide risk in depressed patients
- **Treatment implications**: Comorbid patients may benefit from treatments targeting both conditions (SSRIs, SNRIs, CBT adapted for both)

### When to Use GAD-7 Alongside PHQ-9

- Routinely in depression evaluations, given >50% comorbidity rates
- When a patient presents with somatic complaints (headaches, GI distress, chronic pain) that may have an anxiety component
- When PHQ-9 Item 5 (appetite), Item 3 (sleep), or Item 4 (fatigue) are elevated, as these overlap with anxiety
- During treatment monitoring, to track both symptom domains simultaneously
- When initial treatment for depression shows partial response (unaddressed anxiety may explain the gap)

## Treatment Response Monitoring

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| Response | ≥50% reduction from baseline GAD-7 score |
| Partial response | 25-49% reduction from baseline |
| Non-response | <25% reduction from baseline |
| Remission | Total score < 5 |

## Monitoring Schedule

- **Baseline**: At initial assessment
- **Acute treatment phase**: Every 2-4 weeks
- **Continuation phase**: Monthly for 6-12 months after response
- **Maintenance phase**: Every 3-6 months if ongoing treatment

## Limitations

- Does not differentiate between specific anxiety disorders (GAD vs. panic vs. social anxiety)
- Self-report format subject to response and recall bias
- 2-week window may not capture episodic anxiety (e.g., panic attacks occurring monthly)
- May underperform in older adults who express anxiety somatically rather than cognitively
- Not validated for children under 12 (use age-appropriate measures)

**Sources**: Spitzer et al. (2006), Kroenke et al. (2007), NICE CG113 (2011, updated 2020).
