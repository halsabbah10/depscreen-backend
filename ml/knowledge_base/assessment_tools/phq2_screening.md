# PHQ-2: Two-Item Depression Screener

## Overview

The PHQ-2 is an ultra-brief depression screener consisting of the first two items of the PHQ-9. It targets the two cardinal symptoms of Major Depressive Disorder: depressed mood and anhedonia. Its brevity makes it ideal for routine screening in primary care, emergency departments, and population-level screening programs.

## Items

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following:

| Item | Symptom |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Little interest or pleasure in doing things (anhedonia) |
| 2 | Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless (depressed mood) |

## Scoring

Each item scored 0-3:
- **0** = Not at all
- **1** = Several days
- **2** = More than half the days
- **3** = Nearly every day

**Total score range: 0-6**

## Recommended Cutoff and Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| 0-2 | Screen negative | No further depression screening required at this visit; rescreen per guidelines |
| 3-6 | Screen positive | Administer full PHQ-9 to assess severity and symptom profile |

**Optimal cutoff: ≥ 3**

## Psychometric Properties

- **Sensitivity**: 83% at cutoff ≥ 3 (for detecting MDD)
- **Specificity**: 92% at cutoff ≥ 3
- **Positive likelihood ratio**: 10.1
- **Negative likelihood ratio**: 0.19
- **Area under the ROC curve**: 0.93

These properties make the PHQ-2 an effective rule-out screener. A negative PHQ-2 substantially reduces the probability of MDD, making it a strong first-stage filter.

## When to Use the PHQ-2

### Ideal Use Cases
- **Primary care**: Routine screening at annual wellness visits or new-patient intakes
- **Emergency departments**: Quick screen when depression is not the presenting complaint
- **Population health programs**: Large-scale screening where administration time is critical
- **Repeated screening**: Settings where frequent re-screening is needed (e.g., prenatal visits)
- **Non-psychiatric specialty clinics**: Cardiology, oncology, neurology where depression is comorbid but not the primary focus

### When NOT to Use PHQ-2 Alone
- When a patient is already known to have depression (use PHQ-9 for monitoring)
- When conducting a diagnostic evaluation (PHQ-2 is not a diagnostic tool)
- When detailed symptom profiling is needed for treatment planning
- When monitoring treatment response (PHQ-9 provides the granularity needed)

## Escalation Protocol

When PHQ-2 score ≥ 3:
1. Administer the full PHQ-9 immediately or at a scheduled follow-up within 1-2 weeks
2. If PHQ-9 confirms moderate or higher severity (≥10), initiate treatment planning
3. Always assess Item 9 (suicidal ideation) on the PHQ-9 regardless of total score
4. Document the screening cascade (PHQ-2 → PHQ-9) in the clinical record

## Comparison with PHQ-9

| Feature | PHQ-2 | PHQ-9 |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Purpose | Screening (case-finding) | Severity assessment and monitoring |
| Items | 2 | 9 |
| Administration time | <1 minute | 2-5 minutes |
| Score range | 0-6 | 0-27 |
| Severity grading | No (binary screen) | Yes (5 levels) |
| Treatment monitoring | Not recommended | Recommended |
| Suicide screening | Not included | Item 9 |

## Key Clinical Point

The PHQ-2 is designed to maximize efficiency in detecting potential depression cases. It is a gateway to the PHQ-9, not a replacement. A positive PHQ-2 should always be followed by a comprehensive assessment. A negative PHQ-2 does not definitively rule out depression in patients with clear clinical indicators, and clinician judgment should override a negative screen when risk factors or clinical presentation suggest otherwise.
