# Major Depressive Disorder — Diagnostic Criteria

## Important Disclaimer

**Screening is not diagnosis.** DepScreen is a screening tool that identifies individuals who may benefit from professional evaluation. Only a qualified clinician can diagnose Major Depressive Disorder through comprehensive clinical assessment including history, mental status examination, medical evaluation, and differential diagnosis.

## DSM-5-TR Diagnostic Criteria for Major Depressive Episode

### Criterion A: Symptom Threshold

**Five or more** of the following nine symptoms present during the same **2-week period**, representing a change from previous functioning. **At least one** must be either (1) depressed mood or (2) anhedonia.

| # | Symptom | Category |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | Depressed mood most of the day, nearly every day | Core (required 1 of 2) |
| A2 | Markedly diminished interest or pleasure (anhedonia) | Core (required 1 of 2) |
| A3 | Significant weight loss/gain or appetite change | Neurovegetative |
| A4 | Insomnia or hypersomnia nearly every day | Neurovegetative |
| A5 | Psychomotor agitation or retardation (observable) | Observable |
| A6 | Fatigue or loss of energy nearly every day | Neurovegetative |
| A7 | Worthlessness or excessive/inappropriate guilt | Cognitive |
| A8 | Diminished ability to think, concentrate, or decide | Cognitive |
| A9 | Recurrent thoughts of death, suicidal ideation, or attempt | Risk |

**Key rules:**
- At least **1 core symptom** (A1 or A2) must be present — 5 neurovegetative symptoms without either core symptom do not meet Criterion A
- Each symptom must be present **nearly every day** during the 2-week period (except A3 weight change and A9 which have different frequency patterns)
- Symptoms must represent a **change from baseline** functioning
- A5 (psychomotor) must be **observable by others**, not merely subjective

### Criterion B: Functional Impairment

The symptoms cause **clinically significant distress or impairment** in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.

- Work/academic performance decline
- Relationship deterioration
- Social withdrawal
- Self-care neglect
- Role functioning impairment (parenting, household management)

### Criterion C: Substance/Medical Exclusion

The episode is **not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance** (drug of abuse, medication) or **another medical condition.**

Common medical mimics:
- Hypothyroidism
- Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
- Anemia
- Cushing's syndrome
- Chronic fatigue syndrome
- Sleep apnea
- Traumatic brain injury

Common substance-related causes:
- Alcohol (depressant effects, withdrawal)
- Corticosteroids
- Interferon-alpha
- Beta-blockers
- Hormonal contraceptives
- Benzodiazepine withdrawal

### Criterion D: Not Better Explained by Psychotic Disorder

The episode is **not better explained by** schizoaffective disorder, schizophrenia, schizophreniform disorder, delusional disorder, or other psychotic spectrum disorders.

### Criterion E: No History of Mania/Hypomania

There has **never been a manic or hypomanic episode.**

- If manic episode history exists → Bipolar I Disorder
- If hypomanic episode history exists → Bipolar II Disorder
- This distinction is critical because treatment differs fundamentally (antidepressant monotherapy can destabilize bipolar disorder)

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Symptom Count | Functional Impact | Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | 5 symptoms (minimum threshold) | Distressing but manageable | Can still function, though with difficulty |
| **Moderate** | Symptoms between mild and severe | Clear functional impairment | Work, relationships, self-care affected |
| **Severe** | Most of the 9 symptoms present | Marked functional impairment | May be unable to work or care for self |

Severity is determined by:
1. Number of symptoms beyond minimum threshold
2. Intensity of each symptom
3. Degree of functional impairment
4. Degree of subjective distress

## Single Episode vs. Recurrent

- **Single episode:** First lifetime occurrence meeting full criteria
- **Recurrent:** Two or more episodes separated by at least 2 consecutive months of not meeting full criteria
- Recurrence is common: ~50% after first episode, ~80% after second, ~90% after third

## Partial vs. Full Remission

- **In partial remission:** Some symptoms present but full criteria no longer met, OR less than 2 months since the end of the episode
- **In full remission:** No significant symptoms for ≥2 consecutive months

## How DepScreen Maps to These Criteria

DepScreen's screening output maps to the diagnostic framework as follows:

- **Symptom detection** → identifies which of the 9 criterion A symptoms may be present based on language analysis
- **Core symptom requirement** → verifies at least one core symptom (depressed mood or anhedonia) is indicated
- **Symptom count** → counts toward the 5/9 threshold
- **Severity estimation** → maps detected symptoms and intensity to mild/moderate/severe range
- **Safety flag** → criterion A9 (suicidal ideation) triggers immediate safety protocol regardless of overall score

**What DepScreen cannot assess:**
- Duration confirmation (requires longitudinal observation)
- Criterion B (functional impairment requires clinical interview)
- Criterion C (medical/substance exclusion requires physical exam and labs)
- Criterion D (psychotic disorder exclusion requires clinical assessment)
- Criterion E (mania history requires comprehensive psychiatric history)

This is why screening results always recommend professional evaluation.

This information is for screening and psychoeducation purposes only and does not constitute a clinical diagnosis.
