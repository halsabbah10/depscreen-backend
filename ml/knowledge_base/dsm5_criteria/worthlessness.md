## Feelings of Worthlessness or Excessive Guilt

Feelings of worthlessness or excessive, inappropriate guilt are one of the nine DSM-5 criteria for Major Depressive Disorder. This symptom goes beyond normal self-criticism or regret and involves persistent, distorted negative beliefs about oneself.

### How It Manifests

People experiencing this symptom may describe:

- A deep belief that they are fundamentally flawed, inadequate, or a burden to others
- Harsh, unrelenting self-criticism that is disproportionate to the situation
- Guilt over minor past mistakes or events that others would consider insignificant
- Feeling personally responsible for negative events that are outside their control
- A belief that they do not deserve good things, happiness, or support
- Interpreting neutral events as evidence of personal failure
- Ruminating on perceived shortcomings for hours at a time

The guilt associated with depression is often described as "excessive" or "inappropriate" because it is not grounded in reality. The person may blame themselves for things that are clearly not their fault, or feel guilty simply for being depressed.

### When It Becomes Concerning

Self-reflection and occasional self-doubt are normal parts of life. These feelings become clinically significant when:

- They are persistent and present nearly every day
- They are clearly disproportionate to actual circumstances
- They dominate the person's thinking and are difficult to redirect
- They lead to withdrawal, self-isolation, or avoidance of help
- They may reach delusional intensity (believing one deserves punishment for imagined wrongdoing)
- They accompany other depressive symptoms such as low mood, fatigue, or changes in sleep and appetite

In severe cases, feelings of worthlessness can contribute to hopelessness and suicidal thinking. If self-critical thoughts feel overwhelming, reaching out to a mental health professional is an important step.
