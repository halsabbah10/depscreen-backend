## Anhedonia (Loss of Interest or Pleasure)

Anhedonia is the second core symptom of Major Depressive Disorder in the DSM-5. It refers to a markedly diminished interest or pleasure in all, or almost all, activities most of the day, nearly every day. Along with depressed mood, at least one of these two core symptoms must be present for a depression screening to be considered positive.

### How It Manifests

Anhedonia is more than just boredom or a temporary loss of motivation. People experiencing this symptom often describe it as:

- No longer enjoying hobbies, social activities, or pastimes that were once pleasurable
- Feeling indifferent toward spending time with friends or loved ones
- A reduced desire for physical intimacy or sexual activity
- Food tasting bland or losing its appeal
- Feeling disconnected from music, art, nature, or other sources of enjoyment
- Going through the motions of daily life without any sense of satisfaction

The key feature is that the loss of pleasure is pervasive. It is not limited to one area of life but tends to affect a broad range of experiences.

### When It Becomes Concerning

It is normal to temporarily lose interest in things during periods of stress or fatigue. Anhedonia becomes clinically significant when:

- It affects most activities, not just one or two
- It persists for two weeks or more
- It leads to social withdrawal or isolation
- The person stops participating in activities they previously valued
- It occurs alongside other symptoms such as depressed mood, fatigue, or changes in sleep

Anhedonia can be one of the more difficult symptoms to recognize because the person may not feel actively sad. Instead, they may simply feel "nothing" or report that life feels colorless.

This information is for screening and awareness purposes only and is not a diagnosis.
