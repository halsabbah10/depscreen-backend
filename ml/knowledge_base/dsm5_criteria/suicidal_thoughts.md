## Recurrent Thoughts of Death or Suicidal Ideation

Recurrent thoughts of death, suicidal ideation, or suicidal behavior are one of the nine DSM-5 criteria for Major Depressive Disorder. This is the most serious symptom and always warrants immediate attention and support.

### How It Manifests

This symptom exists on a spectrum and can include:

- Frequent thoughts about death or dying in general
- A wish to not wake up or a feeling that others would be better off without them
- Passive suicidal ideation: thoughts like "I wish I were dead" without a specific plan
- Active suicidal ideation: thinking about specific methods or making plans
- Suicidal behavior: any action taken with the intent to end one's life

Not everyone who experiences these thoughts will act on them, but all expressions of suicidal thinking should be taken seriously. People may also experience intrusive thoughts about death that feel unwanted and distressing.

### When It Becomes Concerning

Any occurrence of suicidal thoughts is clinically significant and should prompt a conversation with a healthcare provider or crisis counselor. It is especially urgent when:

- Thoughts of suicide become frequent or persistent
- The person begins to develop a specific plan or method
- They start acquiring means to carry out a plan
- They express feelings of being a burden to others or that life is not worth living
- They begin giving away possessions or saying goodbye
- There is a sudden shift from distress to calm (which may indicate a decision has been made)

### If You or Someone You Know Is in Crisis

**Please reach out immediately:**

- **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text **988** (US, available 24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **International Association for Suicide Prevention:** https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
- **Emergency services:** Call **911** (US) or your local emergency number

You are not alone, and help is available right now.

This information is for screening and awareness purposes only and is not a diagnosis.
