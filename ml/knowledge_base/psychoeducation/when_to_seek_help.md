## When to Seek Professional Help

Recognizing when to seek professional support is one of the most important steps in managing depression. Many people wait months or even years before reaching out, often because they minimize their symptoms, feel shame, or believe they should be able to handle things on their own. Seeking help is not a sign of weakness; it is a practical and courageous step toward feeling better.

### Signs It May Be Time to Seek Help

Consider reaching out to a healthcare provider or mental health professional if:

- You have been feeling persistently sad, empty, or hopeless for **two weeks or more**
- You have **lost interest or pleasure** in activities you used to enjoy
- Your symptoms are **interfering with daily life**: work, school, relationships, or self-care
- You are experiencing changes in **sleep, appetite, energy, or concentration** that do not improve
- You feel **worthless, excessively guilty**, or have harsh, unrelenting self-critical thoughts
- You are **withdrawing** from friends, family, or social activities
- You are using **alcohol, drugs, or other substances** to cope with your feelings
- People close to you have expressed **concern** about changes in your behavior or mood

### Seek Help Immediately If:

- You are having **thoughts of death or suicide**
- You are thinking about **harming yourself**
- You feel you are **in crisis** or cannot keep yourself safe

**Crisis resources:**
- **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text **988** (US, 24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **Emergency services:** Call **911** or your local emergency number

### Where to Start

- **Primary care physician:** Your regular doctor can screen for depression, rule out medical causes, and provide referrals.
- **Therapist or counselor:** Look for providers specializing in CBT, interpersonal therapy, or evidence-based approaches for depression.
- **Psychiatrist:** A medical doctor who can evaluate the need for medication.
- **Community mental health centers:** Many offer sliding-scale or free services.
- **Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs):** Many workplaces offer free, confidential short-term counseling.

You do not have to be in crisis to deserve support. If something feels wrong, that is reason enough to reach out.

This information is for educational and awareness purposes only and is not a diagnosis.
