## Safety Resources

If you or someone you know is struggling with suicidal thoughts, self-harm, or a mental health emergency, help is available right now. You do not have to face this alone.

### 24/7 Crisis Lines (United States)

| Resource | Contact | Details |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline** | Call or text **988** | Free, confidential, 24/7. Available in English and Spanish. |
| **Crisis Text Line** | Text **HOME** to **741741** | Free, confidential, 24/7 text-based support. |
| **Veterans Crisis Line** | Call **988**, press **1** | Specialized support for veterans and service members. |
| **Trevor Project** | Call **1-866-488-7386** or text **START** to **678-678** | Crisis support for LGBTQ+ youth. |
| **SAMHSA National Helpline** | Call **1-800-662-4357** | Free referrals and information, 24/7. |
| **Emergency Services** | Call **911** | For immediate danger to life. |

### Finding Ongoing Support

- **NIMH Help for Mental Illnesses:** https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/find-help
- **Psychology Today Therapist Finder:** https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/therapists
- **Open Path Collective:** Affordable therapy ($30-$80/session)
- **NAMI Helpline:** Call **1-800-950-6264** (Mon-Fri, 10am-10pm ET)

**You matter. You deserve help. Reaching out is a sign of strength.**
