## Warning Signs of a Mental Health Crisis

Recognizing warning signs early can be life-saving. The following signs may indicate that someone is in or approaching a mental health crisis, particularly related to suicidal thinking. Not everyone will show the same signs, and some people may hide their distress. Trust your instincts; if something feels wrong, it is better to ask and be wrong than to stay silent.

### Verbal Warning Signs

- Talking about wanting to die or wanting to kill themselves
- Saying things like "I wish I had never been born" or "Everyone would be better off without me"
- Expressing feelings of being trapped, in unbearable pain, or having no reason to live
- Talking about being a burden to others
- Saying goodbye or expressing finality ("I won't be around much longer")

### Behavioral Warning Signs

- Searching online for methods of suicide or acquiring means (firearms, stockpiling medications)
- Giving away prized possessions or putting affairs in order
- Withdrawing from friends, family, and social activities
- Increasing use of alcohol or drugs
- Sleeping too much or too little
- Acting recklessly or engaging in risky behaviors
- Visiting or calling people to say goodbye
- A sudden improvement in mood after a period of deep depression (this may signal that a decision has been made)

### Emotional Warning Signs

- Extreme mood swings
- Expressing hopelessness about the future
- Intense anxiety, agitation, or restlessness
- Feeling humiliated, shamed, or like a failure
- Loss of interest in everything, including things previously cared about
- Rage or desire for revenge

### What to Do

If you notice these warning signs in someone:

1. **Ask directly and compassionately:** "Are you thinking about suicide?"
2. **Listen without judgment.** Do not argue or try to minimize their pain.
3. **Stay with them.** Do not leave someone in crisis alone.
4. **Help connect them to support:** Call **988**, text **741741**, or go to the nearest emergency room.
5. **Remove access to lethal means** if possible.

**If there is immediate danger, call 911 or your local emergency number.**
