## If You Are in Immediate Danger

**Call 999** (Bahrain Emergency Services). Available 24/7. For any situation where there is immediate danger to life, including active suicidal behavior, self-harm in progress, or medical emergency resulting from an attempt.

---

## Crisis Helplines

### Shamsaha Helpline

- **Phone**: **17651421**
- **Availability**: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
- **Cost**: Free
- **Confidential**: Yes
- **Services**: Emotional support, crisis intervention, referral to professional services. Available in Arabic and English. No appointment needed. You do not need to give your name.
- **Who it is for**: Anyone in Bahrain experiencing emotional distress, suicidal thoughts, domestic violence, or who needs someone to talk to.

### Dar Al-Aman (House of Safety)

- **Phone**: **8000 8001**
- **Services**: Protection and support for women and children who are victims of domestic violence, abuse, or exploitation. Includes shelter, legal assistance, and counseling.
- **Note**: If a patient's depression is linked to domestic violence or abuse, this is the appropriate referral for protection services.

### Child Protection Centre

- **Phone**: **998**
- **Services**: Reports of child abuse, neglect, or exploitation. Available for children and adolescents, and for adults reporting concerns about a child.

## Psychiatric Emergency Services

### Salmaniya Medical Complex - Psychiatric Emergency

- **Phone**: **+973 1728 8888**
- **Location**: Salmaniya Medical Complex, Manama, Kingdom of Bahrain
- **Services**: Emergency psychiatric evaluation, crisis stabilization, inpatient admission when indicated. Accident and Emergency (A&E) department provides emergency psychiatric care for Bahrainis and non-Bahrainis alike.
- **Cost**: Free for Bahraini citizens. Non-Bahraini residents are treated at A&E regardless of insurance status in emergencies.
- **When to use**: Active suicidal ideation with plan and intent, psychotic episodes, severe agitation, any presentation requiring emergency psychiatric evaluation.

### Salmaniya Medical Complex - Psychiatric Outpatient Appointments

- **Phone**: **+973 1727 9311**
- **Services**: Scheduled psychiatric evaluations, medication management, outpatient follow-up. Referral from primary care is typically required but not always enforced for mental health concerns.
- **Note**: Wait times can vary. For urgent but non-emergency situations, calling this number allows scheduling a priority appointment.

## Substance Use and Addiction

### Taafi Drug Recovery Centre

- **Phone**: **+973 1730 0978**
- **Services**: Substance use assessment, detoxification, rehabilitation, and aftercare. Serves individuals struggling with drug addiction.
- **Note**: Substance use disorders frequently co-occur with depression. If substance use is identified as a comorbidity, referral to Taafi should be considered alongside psychiatric treatment.

---

## Ministry of Health Mental Health Services

### MOH Mental Health Division

- **Website**: moh.gov.bh/Services/MentalHealth
- **Services**: Coordinates government mental health services across Bahrain. Information on available programs, clinics, and community mental health resources.
- **Primary care pathway**: Patients can discuss mental health concerns with their primary care physician at any health centre. GPs can prescribe basic antidepressants and refer to Salmaniya for specialist psychiatric care.

---

## Private Mental Health Providers

Bahrain has a growing network of private psychiatrists, psychologists, and counselors. Most major private hospitals (Royal Bahrain Hospital, American Mission Hospital, Bahrain Specialist Hospital, King Hamad University Hospital) have psychiatry or psychology departments. Sessions are typically covered by comprehensive health insurance plans, though coverage varies by policy.

When referring patients to private providers, verify:
- The provider is licensed by NHRA (National Health Regulatory Authority).
- The specific service needed (psychiatry for medication, psychology for therapy) matches the provider's credentials.
- Insurance coverage and out-of-pocket costs, as these can be barriers to treatment adherence.

---

## Important Notes for Clinicians

- **Language**: Services are available in Arabic and English. Some patients may express distress more readily in Arabic; ensure interpreter services are available if needed.
- **Confidentiality**: Bahraini patients may have concerns about family members learning about their mental health treatment, particularly in a small, interconnected society. Address confidentiality explicitly during intake.
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Mental health stigma remains significant in Bahrain. Frame referrals in terms of overall health and wellbeing when appropriate.
- **Ramadan considerations**: During Ramadan, medication timing may need adjustment to accommodate fasting schedules. Discuss this proactively with patients.
