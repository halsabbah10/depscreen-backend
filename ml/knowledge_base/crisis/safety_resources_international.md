## Global Crisis Directory

### Find a Helpline (Recommended First Stop)

- **Website**: findahelpline.com
- **Coverage**: 40+ countries with verified, up-to-date helpline information.
- **How to use**: Visit findahelpline.com/countries/ and select your country. The directory lists crisis lines, chat services, and text-based support with availability hours, languages, and contact methods.
- **Why this is recommended**: Individual helpline numbers change over time. This directory is actively maintained and provides the most current information for each country.

### International Association for Suicide Prevention (IASP)

- **Website**: iasp.info
- **Crisis Centres Directory**: iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
- **Services**: Maintains a global directory of crisis centres organized by country. Also provides resources for clinicians, researchers, and suicide prevention advocates.

### Befrienders Worldwide

- **Website**: befrienders.org
- **Coverage**: Member organizations in over 30 countries.
- **Services**: Emotional support for people in distress, including suicidal feelings. Each member organization operates independently with local numbers and hours.
- **Languages**: Varies by country. Many centres offer support in the local language and English.

## Online and Text-Based Support

For people who prefer not to speak on the phone or who need support outside helpline hours:

- **Crisis Text Line (US)**: Text HOME to 741741.
- **Shout (UK)**: Text SHOUT to 85258.
- **7 Cups**: Online chat-based emotional support with trained listeners. 7cups.com.
- **IMAlive**: Online chat-based crisis support. imalive.org.
- **Befrienders Worldwide email support**: Available through member organizations. Check befrienders.org for your country.

## For Clinicians: Cross-Border Considerations

When working with patients who may be traveling, relocating, or have connections in multiple countries:

- Ensure the patient has crisis contact information for both their current location and any locations they travel to frequently.
- Include international resources in the safety plan alongside local ones.
- Be aware that crisis service quality, availability, and legal frameworks vary significantly across countries.
- Some countries have mandatory reporting requirements for suicidal ideation that differ from Bahrain. Discuss this with patients who travel frequently.
- Language barriers can be a critical factor. Identify helplines that offer support in the patient's preferred language.

## Important Notes

- Helpline numbers and availability can change. When providing these resources to patients or including them in safety plans, verify current contact information through findahelpline.com or the organization's website.
- Many helplines now offer chat and text in addition to phone calls, which may be preferable for younger patients or those in situations where a phone call is not private.
- Some helplines are staffed by trained volunteers; others by licensed professionals. Both provide valuable crisis support, but for ongoing clinical management, professional follow-up is essential.

This information is maintained for crisis support and clinical referral. Verify contact details periodically.
